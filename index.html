Essential Hygiene Tips

Practical guidance for maintaining health and comfort during your menstrual cycle

Choose the Right Products

Select menstrual products that suit your flow and lifestyle. Change pads every 4-6 hours and tampons every 4-8 hours to prevent infections.

Maintain Hand Hygiene

Always wash your hands before and after changing menstrual products. Use soap and warm water for at least 20 seconds.

Daily Cleansing

Take regular showers and clean the genital area with mild, unscented soap. Avoid douching as it can disrupt natural pH balance.

Wear Breathable Clothing

Choose cotton underwear and loose-fitting clothes. Avoid tight synthetic fabrics that can trap moisture and bacteria.

Rest and Sleep

Get adequate sleep and rest during your period. Use extra protection at night and consider period underwear for added security.

Nutrition and Hydration

Eat iron-rich foods and stay hydrated. Avoid excessive caffeine and processed foods that may worsen cramps.

When to Consult a Doctor

Seek medical attention if you experience: severe pain that doesn't improve with medication, periods lasting longer than 7 days, very heavy bleeding, irregular cycles, or any unusual symptoms. Regular gynecological check-ups are important for maintaining reproductive health.

Myths vs Facts

Debunking common misconceptions about menstruation with scientific facts

MYTH

"You can't exercise during your period"

FACT

Exercise can actually help reduce menstrual cramps and improve mood through endorphin release. Light to moderate exercise is beneficial during periods.

MYTH

"Periods sync up when women live together"

FACT

Scientific studies have found no evidence for menstrual synchrony. This is likely a case of confirmation bias and coincidence.

MYTH

"You can't swim during your period"

FACT

Water pressure prevents menstrual flow while swimming. Using a tampon or menstrual cup makes swimming perfectly safe and hygienic.

MYTH

"PMS is just psychological"

FACT

PMS is a real medical condition caused by hormonal fluctuations. Symptoms can be physical, emotional, and behavioral, affecting daily life.

MYTH

"You lose a lot of blood during periods"

FACT

The average menstrual flow is only 30-40ml of actual blood (about 2-3 tablespoons) mixed with other fluids over the entire cycle.

MYTH

"Irregular periods are always a problem"

FACT

Cycles can vary from 21-35 days and still be normal. However, sudden changes in cycle pattern should be discussed with a healthcare provider.

Common questions about menstrual health answered by experts

A normal menstrual cycle ranges from 21 to 35 days, with the average being 28 days. The menstrual period typically lasts 3-7 days. However, what's normal varies from person to person, and your cycle may change throughout your life.

Pads should be changed every 4-6 hours, tampons every 4-8 hours (never exceed 8 hours), and menstrual cups can be worn for up to 12 hours depending on your flow. Regular changing prevents bacterial growth and reduces infection risk.
